Introduction

In an increasingly data-driven society, our ability to understand, interpret, and responsibly use data is an important skill set not only for data and computer scientists but every member of the public. Growing data literacy among our citizenry is a shared responsibility, where all members of the campus community can play a critical role in building this competency.

The Data Literacy Toolkit is a research-informed resource to support community college educators and academic librarians in fostering a culture of data literacy across their institutions...

Learning Objectives

  • Define data literacy and articulate its value across the academic community.
  • Identify foundational concepts and competencies required for data-literate thinking and behavior.
  • Promote a campus-wide culture of data literacy through engaging initiatives, activities, and events.
  • Integrate data literacy concepts into instruction and programming in alignment with institutional goals and learner needs.

How to Navigate this Toolkit

This toolkit is organized into six modules designed to introduce the concept of data literacy and how you can grow this skill set among students on your campus.

  1. Module 1: The Foundations of Data Literacy – Core concepts of data literacy and their importance for information professionals.
  2. Module 2: Building a Data Culture on Your Campus – Strategies to engage stakeholders and promote a data-savvy mindset.
  3. Module 3: Incorporating Data Literacy Instruction – Introduction to embedding data literacy in curricula and activities.
  4. Module 4: Data and Data Sources – Teaching about data types, sources, and quality evaluation.
  5. Module 5: Making Data Insightful and Actionable – Interpretation, analysis, and communication of data.
  6. Module 6: Managing Data Responsibly and Ethically – Addressing privacy, bias, and ethical data use.

By using this toolkit, you will be empowered to enhance data fluency across your institution!
